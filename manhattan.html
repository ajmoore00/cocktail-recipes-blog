Manhattan

Ingredients

  • 2 oz rye or bourbon whiskey
  • 1 oz sweet vermouth
  • 2-3 dashes Angostura bitters
  • Maraschino cherry or orange twist for garnish
  • Ice

Instructions

  1. Fill a mixing glass with ice.
  2. Combine whiskey, sweet vermouth, and bitters.
  3. Stir until well chilled.
  4. Strain into a chilled cocktail glass.
  5. Garnish with a maraschino cherry or orange twist and enjoy.

Variations

  • Perfect Manhattan: Use equal parts sweet and dry vermouth.
  • Dry Manhattan: Use only dry vermouth for a less sweet drink.
  • Black Manhattan: Swap vermouth with Averna or another amaro for a richer flavor.

History

The Manhattan cocktail was created in the early 1870s at the Manhattan Club in New York City. It was reportedly invented by Dr. Iain Marshall for a banquet hosted by Lady Randolph Churchill.

The drink's success at the event made it popular, leading to its name. Over time, it has become a staple in cocktail culture.
