Margarita

Ingredients

  • 2 oz tequila
  • 1 oz lime juice
  • 1 oz triple sec (or cointreau)
  • Slice of lime
  • Salt for rimming the glass
  • Ice

Instructions

  1. Rub the rim of a glass with a lime wedge, then dip in salt to coat.
  2. In a shaker, combine tequila, lime juice, and triple sec.
  3. Add ice and shake well until chilled.
  4. Strain into the prepared glass over ice.
  5. Garnish with a lime wedge and enjoy!

Variations

  • Spicy Margarita: Add a slice of jalapeño to the shaker for a kick.
  • Frozen Margarita: Blend all ingredients with ice for a slushy version.
  • Strawberry Margarita: Add fresh strawberries to the mix for a fruity twist.

History

While the origins of the Margarita seem to be unknown, there are several competing stories as to its creation.

Carlos 'Danny' Herrera (1938): A bartender in Tijuana, Mexico, is said to have created the Margarita for a showgirl allergic to other spirits, mixing tequila, lime juice, and triple sec.

Margarita Sames (1948): A Dallas socialite reportedly invented the drink at her vacation home in Acapulco during a party, which later gained popularity through hotelier Tommy Hilton.

Tequila Daisy (1930s): Some believe the Margarita evolved from the 'Tequila Daisy,' a cocktail made with tequila, citrus, and liqueur, with 'margarita' meaning 'daisy' in Spanish.

Hussong’s Cantina: Another claim comes from Don Carlos Orozco at Hussong’s in Ensenada, who allegedly created it for Margarita Henkel in the late 1930s.
